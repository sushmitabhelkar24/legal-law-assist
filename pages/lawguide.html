Lost Phone – Legal Steps

1. File an FIR at the nearest police station.

2. Block SIM card immediately.

3. Provide IMEI number to police.

Relevant Law: IT Act & Theft under IPC.

Cybercrime Complaint

1. Report at National Cyber Crime Portal.

2. Save evidence (screenshots).

Relevant Law: Information Technology Act, 2000.

Consumer Complaint

1. Send legal notice to company.

2. File complaint in Consumer Court.

Relevant Law: Consumer Protection Act.

Property Dispute

1. Verify ownership documents.

2. Send legal notice to opposite party.

3. File civil suit in court if unresolved.

Relevant Law: Transfer of Property Act.

Divorce Procedure

1. File divorce petition in Family Court.

2. Submit marriage proof documents.

3. Attend mediation sessions if required.

Relevant Law: Hindu Marriage Act / Special Marriage Act.

Child Custody Case

1. File custody petition in Family Court.

2. Provide child's welfare details.

3. Court decides based on best interest of child.

Relevant Law: Guardian and Wards Act.

Domestic Violence Complaint

1. File complaint at police station.

2. Approach Protection Officer.

3. Seek protection order from court.

Relevant Law: Domestic Violence Act, 2005.

Cheque Bounce Case

1. Send legal notice within 30 days.

2. Wait 15 days for payment.

3. File complaint in Magistrate Court.

Relevant Law: Negotiable Instruments Act, Section 138.

Bank Fraud Complaint

1. Inform bank immediately.

2. File police complaint.

3. Report on cybercrime portal.

Relevant Law: IT Act & IPC Fraud Sections.

Online Shopping Fraud

1. Contact customer support.

2. Send written complaint.

3. File case in Consumer Court.

Relevant Law: Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

Workplace Harassment

1. File complaint to Internal Committee.

2. Submit written evidence.

3. Approach Labour Court if unresolved.

Relevant Law: POSH Act, 2013.

Wrongful Termination

1. Review employment contract.

2. Send legal notice to employer.

3. File complaint in Labour Court.

Relevant Law: Industrial Disputes Act.

Road Accident Compensation

1. File FIR immediately.

2. Collect medical reports.

3. File claim before MACT court.

Relevant Law: Motor Vehicles Act.

Immigration Visa Issue

1. Verify visa rejection reason.

2. Submit appeal if allowed.

3. Consult immigration lawyer.

Relevant Law: Immigration & Visa Regulations.

Tax Notice from Department

1. Read notice carefully.

2. Gather income documents.

3. Respond within deadline.

Relevant Law: Income Tax Act.

Trademark Registration

1. Search trademark availability.

2. File application online.

3. Respond to objections if any.

Relevant Law: Trade Marks Act, 1999.
